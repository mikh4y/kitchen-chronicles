1. Breakfast: Bubur Ayam (Chicken Congee)
Ingredients:
- 1 cup rice
- 4 cups chicken broth
- 1 boneless, skinless chicken breast
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
- Toppings: shredded chicken, fried shallots, chopped green onions, soy sauce, lime wedges

Instructions:
- In a pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil.
- Add the rice, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Stir well.
- Reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for about 30 minutes or until the rice is soft and the congee is thickened.
- Meanwhile, boil the chicken breast in a separate pot until cooked through. Shred the chicken.
- Serve the congee hot, topped with shredded chicken, fried shallots, chopped green onions, a drizzle of soy sauce, and a squeeze of lime juice.

2. Appetizer: Siomay (Steamed Dumplings)
Ingredients:
- 250 grams fish fillet (such as mackerel or white fish)
- 150 grams shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 100 grams tapioca flour
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon fish sauce
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Cabbage leaves (for steaming)
- Peanut sauce for serving

Instructions:
- In a food processor, blend the fish fillet and shrimp until smooth.
- Transfer the mixture to a bowl and add the tapioca flour, minced garlic, fish sauce, sugar, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine.
- Scoop spoonfuls of the mixture and shape them into small dumplings.
- Steam the dumplings on a bed of cabbage leaves for about 15-20 minutes or until cooked through.
- Serve the siomay with peanut sauce for dipping.

3. Lunch: Sate Padang (Padang-style Satay)
Ingredients:
- 500 grams beef (such as sirloin or tenderloin), thinly sliced
- Bamboo skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 shallots, minced
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon coriander powder
- 1 teaspoon cumin powder
- 1 teaspoon chili powder (optional for spicier flavor)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Peanut sauce for serving

Instructions:
- In a bowl, mix together the minced garlic, minced shallots, vegetable oil, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, chili powder, salt, and pepper to make a marinade.
- Add the beef slices to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Let them marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor.
- Thread the marinated beef slices onto the soaked bamboo skewers.
- Grill the satay over medium heat, turning occasionally, until cooked to your desired doneness.
- Serve the sate padang hot with peanut sauce for dipping.

4. Snack: Perkedel Jagung (Corn Fritters)
Ingredients:
- 2 cups fresh corn kernels (or canned corn, drained)
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 2 eggs
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 green onions, finely chopped
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:
- In a bowl, combine the corn kernels, all-purpose flour, eggs, minced garlic, chopped green onions, salt

, and pepper. Mix well until the ingredients are fully incorporated.
- Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat.
- Drop spoonfuls of the corn mixture into the hot oil, shaping them into fritters.
- Fry the fritters until golden brown on both sides.
- Remove the fritters from the skillet and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
- Serve the perkedel jagung warm as a snack.

5. Dinner: Ayam Goreng Kalasan (Kalasan-style Fried Chicken)
Ingredients:
- 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 shallots, minced
- 2 teaspoons coriander powder
- 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
- Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:
- In a bowl, mix together the minced garlic, minced shallots, coriander powder, turmeric powder, salt, sugar, and tamarind paste to make a marinade.
- Rub the chicken pieces with the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Let them marinate for at least 2 hours, or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor.
- Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or skillet over medium heat.
- Fry the marinated chicken pieces until golden brown and cooked through, flipping occasionally.
- Remove the fried chicken from the pan and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
- Serve the ayam goreng Kalasan hot as a main dish.

6. Appetizer: Bakwan Sayur (Vegetable Fritters)
Ingredients:
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup water
- 1 egg
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon pepper
- Assorted vegetables (such as shredded cabbage, carrots, and bean sprouts)
- Oil for frying
- Sweet chili sauce for serving

Instructions:
- In a bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, water, egg, baking powder, salt, and pepper until smooth.
- Add the shredded vegetables to the batter and mix well to coat them evenly.
- Heat oil in a skillet or deep fryer over medium-high heat.
- Drop spoonfuls of the vegetable batter into the hot oil, shaping them into fritters.
- Fry the fritters until golden brown and crispy, turning them occasionally.
- Remove the fritters from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
- Serve the bakwan sayur warm with sweet chili sauce for dipping.

7. Dessert: Es Cendol
Ingredients:
- 1 cup rice flour
- 2 tablespoons pandan juice (from pandan leaves)
- 3 cups coconut milk
- 1/2 cup palm sugar syrup
- Shaved ice
- Red beans (cooked and chilled)
- Cendol (green rice flour jelly)
- Coconut flesh (optional)

Instructions:
- In a bowl, mix the rice flour and pandan juice until well combined to make the cendol dough.
- Push the cendol dough through a cendol press or a colander with small holes into a pot of boiling water.
- Cook the cendol in boiling water for about 5 minutes, then drain and rinse with cold water.
- In a serving glass or bowl, layer shaved ice, red beans, cendol, and coconut flesh.
- Pour coconut milk and palm sugar syrup over the ingredients.
- Serve the es cendol chilled as a refreshing dessert.

8. Dinner: Nasi Uduk (Coconut Rice)
Ingredients:
- 2 cups jasmine rice
- 

2 cups coconut milk
- 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised
- 2 kaffir lime leaves
- Salt to taste
- Fried shallots for garnish

Instructions:
- Rinse the jasmine rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
- In a rice cooker or a pot, combine the rice, coconut milk, lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and salt.
- Cook the rice according to the rice cooker's instructions or simmer it over low heat until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
- Remove the lemongrass and kaffir lime leaves.
- Fluff the rice with a fork and transfer it to a serving dish.
- Garnish with fried shallots.
- Serve the nasi uduk hot as a side dish.

9. Dessert: Pisang Goreng (Banana Fritters)
Ingredients:
- Ripe bananas, peeled and sliced
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/4 cup rice flour
- 1/4 cup cornstarch
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 3/4 cup water
- Oil for frying
- Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:
- In a bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, rice flour, cornstarch, salt, baking powder, sugar, and water until smooth.
- Dip the banana slices into the batter, coating them evenly.
- Heat oil in a skillet or deep fryer over medium heat.
- Fry the battered banana slices until golden brown and crispy, turning them occasionally.
- Remove the banana fritters from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
- Dust the pisang goreng with powdered sugar.
- Serve the banana fritters warm as a sweet treat.

10. Dinner: Gado-Gado (Indonesian Vegetable Salad)
Ingredients:
- Assorted blanched vegetables (such as cabbage, bean sprouts, green beans, carrots)
- Tofu, cut into cubes
- Boiled eggs, halved
- Cucumber, sliced
- Bean curd crackers (kerupuk)
- Peanut Sauce:
  - 1 cup roasted peanuts, finely ground
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1 tablespoon tamarind juice
  - 1 tablespoon sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  - Salt and sugar to taste
  - Water (as needed for desired consistency)

Instructions:
- Arrange the blanched vegetables, tofu, boiled eggs, cucumber, and bean curd crackers on a plate.
- In a bowl, combine the ground peanuts, minced garlic, tamarind juice, sweet soy sauce, salt, and sugar.
- Gradually add water and stir until you achieve the desired consistency for the peanut sauce.
- Drizzle the peanut sauce over the vegetables and garnish with additional crushed peanuts.
- Serve the gado-gado immediately.
