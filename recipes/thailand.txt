1. Breakfast: Khao Tom (Thai Rice Soup)
Ingredients:
- 1 cup jasmine rice
- 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
- 200 grams ground pork or chicken
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- Green onions and cilantro for garnish

Instructions:
- Rinse the jasmine rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
- In a pot, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a boil.
- Add the rinsed rice to the boiling broth and cook for about 15-20 minutes or until the rice is soft and has a porridge-like consistency.
- In a separate pan, cook the ground pork or chicken over medium heat until fully cooked.
- Add the minced garlic, soy sauce, fish sauce, and sesame oil to the cooked meat. Stir well to combine.
- Add the seasoned meat to the rice soup and stir.
- Simmer for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
- Serve the khao tom hot, garnished with green onions and cilantro.

2. Appetizer: Tom Yum Goong (Spicy Shrimp Soup)
Ingredients:
- 10-12 medium-sized shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
- 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised
- 3-4 kaffir lime leaves
- 3 slices galangal
- 4-6 bird's eye chilies, crushed
- 1 small onion, sliced
- 1 tomato, sliced
- 200 grams mushrooms, sliced
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions:
- In a pot, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a boil.
- Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, and crushed bird's eye chilies to the boiling broth. Let it simmer for a few minutes to infuse the flavors.
- Add the sliced onion, tomato, and mushrooms to the pot.
- Cook for a few more minutes until the vegetables are tender.
- Season the soup with fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar. Adjust the seasoning to your taste.
- Add the shrimp to the pot and cook until they turn pink and are fully cooked.
- Remove the pot from the heat and discard the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal slices.
- Serve the tom yum goong hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves.

3. Lunch: Pad Thai (Thai Stir-Fried Noodles)
Ingredients:
- 8 ounces rice noodles
- 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 200 grams shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 1 cup bean sprouts
- 2 green onions, chopped
- Crushed peanuts for garnish
- Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:
- Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
- In a small bowl, whisk together the tamarind paste, fish sauce, soy sauce, and sugar. Set aside.
- Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large pan over medium heat.
- Saut√© the minced garlic until fragrant.
- Push the garlic to one side of the pan and pour the beaten eggs into the empty space. Scram

ble the eggs until they are almost cooked.
- Add the shrimp to the pan and stir-fry until they turn pink and are fully cooked.
- Add the cooked rice noodles to the pan and pour the tamarind sauce mixture over them.
- Toss everything together to coat the noodles evenly with the sauce.
- Add the bean sprouts and chopped green onions to the pan and stir-fry for a minute or two until the bean sprouts are slightly wilted.
- Remove from heat and garnish with crushed peanuts.
- Serve the pad Thai hot, with lime wedges on the side.

4. Snack: Tod Mun Pla (Thai Fish Cakes)
Ingredients:
- 500 grams white fish fillets, deboned and roughly chopped
- 1 tablespoon red curry paste
- 1 egg
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 2 kaffir lime leaves, finely shredded
- 1/4 cup green beans, finely chopped
- Vegetable oil for frying
- Sweet chili sauce for dipping

Instructions:
- In a food processor, blend the fish fillets until they form a smooth paste.
- Transfer the fish paste to a mixing bowl and add the red curry paste, egg, fish sauce, sugar, kaffir lime leaves, and green beans. Mix well to combine.
- Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or wok over medium heat.
- Using a spoon or your hands, shape the fish mixture into small patties or balls.
- Carefully drop the fish cakes into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and cooked through.
- Remove the fish cakes from the oil and drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
- Serve the tod mun pla hot, with sweet chili sauce for dipping.

5. Dinner: Green Curry Chicken
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons green curry paste
- 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
- 1 cup chicken broth
- 500 grams chicken breast, sliced
- 1 eggplant, sliced
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar
- Basil leaves for garnish
- Cooked jasmine rice for serving

Instructions:
- In a wok or large pan, heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat.
- Add the green curry paste to the pan and stir-fry for a minute to release its flavors.
- Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth, and stir well to combine.
- Add the sliced chicken breast and simmer until the chicken is cooked through.
- Add the eggplant and red bell pepper to the pan, and continue to cook until the vegetables are tender.
- Season the curry with fish sauce and palm sugar, adjusting the flavors to your liking.
- Garnish with fresh basil leaves.
- Serve the green curry chicken hot with jasmine rice.

6. Appetizer: Yum Woon Sen (Thai Glass Noodle Salad)
Ingredients:
- 100 grams glass noodles (bean thread noodles)
- 200 grams ground pork or chicken
- 2 tablespoons lime juice
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 tablespoon chili paste or chili flakes
- 1 small onion, thinly sliced
- 1 tomato, diced
- 1 cucumber, sliced
- Handful of fresh cilantro leaves
- Crushed peanuts for garnish

Instructions:
- Cook the glass noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
- In a small bowl, mix together the lime juice, fish sauce, sugar, and chili paste or chili flakes.
- In a pan, cook the ground pork or chicken over medium heat until fully cooked. Set aside to cool.
- In a large

 mixing bowl, combine the cooked glass noodles, cooked ground meat, sliced onion, diced tomato, sliced cucumber, and fresh cilantro leaves.
- Pour the dressing over the noodle mixture and toss everything together until well coated.
- Garnish with crushed peanuts.
- Serve the yum woon sen chilled or at room temperature.

7. Breakfast: Khanom Krok (Coconut Pancakes)
Ingredients:
- 1 cup rice flour
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1/2 cup water
- 1/4 cup sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- Vegetable oil for greasing

Instructions:
- In a mixing bowl, whisk together the rice flour, coconut milk, water, sugar, and salt until smooth.
- Heat a khanom krok pan or a mini pancake pan over medium heat and lightly grease each indentation with vegetable oil.
- Pour the batter into the indentations, filling them about three-quarters full.
- Cook for a few minutes until the bottoms are golden brown and set.
- Flip the pancakes using a skewer or toothpick, and cook for another minute or two until the other side is also golden brown.
- Remove the pancakes from the pan and repeat with the remaining batter.
- Serve the khanom krok pancakes warm as a delicious breakfast treat.

8. Snack: Mango Sticky Rice
Ingredients:
- 1 cup sticky rice (glutinous rice)
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1/2 cup sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 2 ripe mangoes, sliced
- Toasted sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:
- Rinse the sticky rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
- Soak the rice in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
- Drain the soaked rice and steam it in a bamboo steamer or a steamer basket lined with cheesecloth for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and sticky.
- In a small saucepan, heat the coconut milk, sugar, and salt over low heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
- Remove the cooked sticky rice from the steamer and transfer it to a bowl.
- Pour half of the coconut milk mixture over the rice and gently stir to coat the rice with the mixture.
- Let the rice sit for a few minutes to absorb the coconut milk.
- Serve the sticky rice with the sliced mangoes, drizzling the remaining coconut milk mixture on top.
- Garnish with toasted sesame seeds.

9. Dinner: Massaman Curry
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons massaman curry paste
- 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
- 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
- 500 grams beef or chicken, cubed
- 1 onion, sliced
- 1 potato, cubed
- 1 carrot, sliced
- 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar
- Roasted peanuts for garnish
- Cooked jasmine rice for serving

Instructions:
- In a wok or large pan, heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat.
- Add the massaman curry paste to the pan and stir-fry for a minute to release its flavors.
- Pour in the coconut milk and chicken or vegetable broth, and stir well to combine.
- Add the cubed beef or chicken to the pan and simmer until the meat is cooked through.
- Add the sliced onion, cubed potato, and sliced carrot to the pan, and continue to cook until the vegetables are tender.
- In a small bowl, mix together the tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar. Add this mixture to the pan and stir well.


- Simmer for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
- Garnish with roasted peanuts.
- Serve the massaman curry hot with jasmine rice.
