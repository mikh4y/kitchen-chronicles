1. Breakfast: Tapsilog (Marinated Beef with Garlic Fried Rice and Fried Egg)
Ingredients:
- 1 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons calamansi juice or lemon juice
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Garlic fried rice
- Fried eggs

Instructions:
- In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, calamansi juice, minced garlic, sugar, salt, and pepper to make a marinade.
- Add the sliced beef to the marinade and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
- Heat a pan over medium-high heat and cook the marinated beef until browned and cooked through.
- Serve the tapsilog with garlic fried rice and fried eggs.

2. Appetizer: Lumpiang Shanghai (Filipino Spring Rolls)
Ingredients:
- 1 pound ground pork
- 1 cup carrots, finely grated
- 1 cup onions, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon black pepper
- Lumpia wrappers
- Vegetable oil for frying
- Sweet chili sauce for dipping

Instructions:
- In a bowl, combine the ground pork, grated carrots, chopped onions, minced garlic, soy sauce, and black pepper.
- Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of a lumpia wrapper.
- Roll the wrapper tightly, tucking in the sides as you go.
- Seal the edge of the wrapper with a bit of water.
- Repeat with the remaining filling and wrappers.
- Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
- Fry the lumpia rolls in batches until golden brown and crispy.
- Serve the lumpiang Shanghai hot with sweet chili sauce for dipping.

3. Lunch: Chicken Adobo
Ingredients:
- 2 pounds chicken pieces (legs, thighs, or wings)
- 1/2 cup soy sauce
- 1/2 cup vinegar
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tablespoon cooking oil
- Cooked rice for serving

Instructions:
- In a large bowl, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, black peppercorns, and bay leaves.
- Add the chicken pieces to the bowl and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
- Heat the cooking oil in a pot over medium heat.
- Remove the chicken from the marinade and reserve the marinade.
- Sear the chicken pieces in the hot oil until browned on all sides.
- Pour in the reserved marinade and bring it to a boil.
- Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
- Serve the chicken adobo hot with cooked rice.

4. Snack: Halo-Halo (Mixed Fruits and Shaved Ice Dessert)
Ingredients:
- Assorted fruits (such as bananas, jackfruit, sweet beans, nata de coco, and palm seeds)
- Shaved ice
- Sweetened condensed milk
- Leche flan (caramel custard)
- Ube (purple yam) ice cream
- Pinipig (crispy rice flakes)
- Toasted rice flakes
- Kaong (sugar palm fruit)
- Red and green gulaman (agar jelly)
- Coconut strips

Instructions:
- In a tall glass or bowl, layer the assorted fruits.
- Add a generous amount of shaved ice on top.
- Drizzle sweetened condensed milk over the shaved ice.


- Add small slices of leche flan and a scoop of ube ice cream.
- Sprinkle pinipig and toasted rice flakes on top.
- Add kaong, gulaman, and coconut strips as desired.
- Mix everything together before eating and enjoy the refreshing halo-halo.

5. Dinner: Sinigang na Baboy (Pork Sour Soup)
Ingredients:
- 1 pound pork ribs or pork belly, cut into serving pieces
- 8 cups water
- 1 medium onion, quartered
- 2 tomatoes, quartered
- 2 cups tamarind juice (from fresh tamarind or tamarind paste)
- 1 cup green beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 2 cups kangkong (water spinach) or spinach leaves
- Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:
- In a large pot, bring the water to a boil.
- Add the pork ribs or pork belly to the pot and simmer for about 1 hour or until the meat is tender.
- Add the quartered onion and tomatoes to the pot.
- Pour in the tamarind juice and bring the soup to a boil.
- Add the green beans and cook for a few minutes until they are tender.
- Stir in the kangkong or spinach leaves and cook for another minute until wilted.
- Season the sinigang with salt and pepper to taste.
- Serve the sinigang na baboy hot with steamed rice.

6. Appetizer: Tokwa't Baboy (Fried Tofu and Pork)
Ingredients:
- 1 block firm tofu, cut into cubes
- 1/2 pound pork belly, boiled and cut into cubes
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons vinegar
- 1 small onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons cooking oil
- Chili peppers (optional)
- Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:
- Heat the cooking oil in a pan over medium heat.
- Fry the tofu cubes until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the pan and set aside.
- In the same pan, sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant.
- Add the boiled pork belly cubes to the pan and cook until browned.
- Pour in the soy sauce and vinegar. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
- Season with salt and pepper to taste.
- Add the fried tofu cubes to the pan and stir everything together.
- Serve the tokwa't baboy hot as an appetizer.

7. Breakfast: Champorado (Chocolate Rice Porridge)
Ingredients:
- 1 cup glutinous rice
- 4 cups water
- 3/4 cup cocoa powder
- 1/2 cup sugar
- Condensed milk for serving
- Tuyo (dried fish) for serving (optional)

Instructions:
- In a pot, combine the glutinous rice and water. Bring it to a boil.
- Reduce the heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the rice is cooked and the mixture thickens.
- In a separate bowl, dissolve the cocoa powder and sugar in a small amount of water to make a paste.
- Add the cocoa paste to the pot and stir well to combine.
- Continue cooking the champorado for another 5 minutes until thick and smooth.
- Serve the champorado hot with a drizzle of condensed milk and optional dried fish.

8. Snack: Bibingka (Rice Cake)
Ingredients:
- 2 cups rice flour
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1/2 cup water
- 3/4 cup sugar


- Banana leaves (optional)
- Grated coconut for serving

Instructions:
- Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
- In a bowl, combine the rice flour, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt.
- In a separate bowl, mix the coconut milk, water, and sugar until well combined.
- Gradually add the wet mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring until smooth.
- If using banana leaves, cut them into squares and lightly grease them with oil. Line the bottom of a baking pan with the banana leaves.
- Pour the batter into the pan, spreading it evenly.
- Bake the bibingka in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
- Serve the bibingka warm with grated coconut on top.

9. Dinner: Kare-Kare (Oxtail and Vegetable Stew in Peanut Sauce)
Ingredients:
- 2 pounds oxtail, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 cup peanut butter
- 1/2 cup ground toasted rice
- 4 cups beef broth
- 2 tablespoons annatto seeds (atsuete)
- 1 medium onion, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 cups eggplant, sliced
- 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
- Bagoong (shrimp paste) for serving
- Cooking oil
- Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:
- In a pot, boil the oxtail pieces in water for about 1 hour or until tender. Drain and set aside.
- In a separate pan, heat some cooking oil and sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant.
- Add the boiled oxtail to the pan and cook for a few minutes to brown.
- Dissolve the annatto seeds in the beef broth and add it to the pan.
- Stir in the peanut butter and ground toasted rice. Mix well to combine.
- Simmer the kare-kare for about 30 minutes, adding more broth if needed to achieve the desired consistency.
- Add the sliced eggplant and string beans to the pot and cook until the vegetables are tender.
- Season the kare-kare with salt and pepper to taste.
- Serve the kare-kare hot with bagoong on the side and steamed rice.

10. Dessert: Leche Flan (Caramel Custard)
Ingredients:
- 10 egg yolks
- 1 can condensed milk
- 1 can evaporated milk
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 cup sugar for caramel

Instructions:
- In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract until well combined.
- In a separate pan, melt the sugar over medium heat until it turns into caramel, stirring constantly.
- Pour the caramel into individual llanera (oval-shaped molds) or any heatproof molds.
- Gently pour the egg mixture over the caramel in the molds.
- Cover each mold tightly with aluminum foil.
- Steam the leche flan for about 30-40 minutes or until set.
- Remove the molds from the steamer and let them cool.
- Once cooled, refrigerate the leche flan for at least 2 hours or overnight.
- To serve, invert each mold onto a plate, allowing the caramel to flow over the custard.
- Serve the leche flan chilled as a delightful dessert.
