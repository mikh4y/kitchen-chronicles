1. Breakfast: Pho Bo (Beef Noodle Soup)
Ingredients:
- 500 grams beef (brisket, flank steak, or sirloin), thinly sliced
- 300 grams rice noodles
- 2 liters beef broth
- 1 onion, sliced
- 2-inch piece of ginger, sliced
- 2-3 star anise pods
- 2-3 cloves
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- Fresh herbs (Thai basil, cilantro, mint) for garnish
- Bean sprouts, lime wedges, and sliced chili peppers for serving

Instructions:
- In a large pot, add the beef broth, onion, ginger, star anise, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil and simmer for 30 minutes to infuse the flavors.
- Meanwhile, cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
- Strain the broth to remove the solids, and return the broth to the pot. Add the fish sauce and bring it back to a simmer.
- Divide the cooked rice noodles among serving bowls. Top with thinly sliced beef.
- Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and beef, allowing the heat to cook the beef.
- Serve the pho hot, garnished with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime wedges, and sliced chili peppers.

2. Appetizer: Goi Cuon (Fresh Spring Rolls)
Ingredients:
- Rice paper wrappers
- Cooked shrimp, sliced in half lengthwise
- Rice vermicelli noodles, cooked and drained
- Lettuce leaves
- Fresh herbs (mint, cilantro, Thai basil)
- Bean sprouts
- Thinly sliced cucumber
- Thinly sliced carrot
- Peanut dipping sauce for serving

Instructions:
- Fill a large bowl with warm water. Dip one rice paper wrapper into the water and let it soften for a few seconds.
- Place the softened rice paper on a clean surface.
- Layer a few lettuce leaves, a handful of rice vermicelli noodles, fresh herbs, bean sprouts, sliced cucumber, and sliced carrot on the lower half of the rice paper.
- Fold the sides of the rice paper over the filling, then roll it up tightly.
- Repeat with the remaining ingredients.
- Serve the fresh spring rolls with peanut dipping sauce.

3. Lunch: Banh Mi (Vietnamese Sandwich)
Ingredients:
- Baguette or Vietnamese-style bread rolls
- Thinly sliced grilled or roasted meat (pork, chicken, or beef)
- Pickled carrots and daikon radish
- Sliced cucumber
- Fresh cilantro leaves
- Sliced jalapeno peppers
- Mayonnaise and/or pate
- Maggi sauce or soy sauce

Instructions:
- Slice the baguette or bread rolls in half lengthwise.
- Spread mayonnaise and/or pate on the inside of the bread.
- Layer the sliced meat, pickled carrots and daikon radish, sliced cucumber, fresh cilantro leaves, and sliced jalapeno peppers on the bread.
- Drizzle with Maggi sauce or soy sauce.
- Close the sandwich and press it lightly.
- Serve the banh mi sandwiches as a delicious lunch option.

4. Snack: Banh Xeo (Vietnamese Savory Pancakes)
Ingredients:
- 1 cup rice flour
- 1/2 cup coconut milk
- 1/2 cup water
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1/2 cup sliced pork belly or shrimp
- Bean sprouts
- Sliced green onions
- Fresh herbs (mint

, cilantro, Thai basil)
- Fish sauce or dipping sauce for serving

Instructions:
- In a mixing bowl, whisk together the rice flour, coconut milk, water, and turmeric powder until smooth.
- Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add a tablespoon of oil.
- Pour a ladleful of the batter into the skillet, tilting it to spread the batter evenly.
- Add a few slices of pork belly or shrimp, some bean sprouts, and sliced green onions to one half of the pancake.
- Cover the skillet and cook for a few minutes until the bottom is golden and crispy.
- Fold the pancake in half to cover the filling, then transfer it to a plate.
- Repeat with the remaining batter and filling.
- Serve the banh xeo pancakes hot, accompanied by fish sauce or dipping sauce.

5. Dinner: Ca Kho To (Caramelized Fish in Clay Pot)
Ingredients:
- 500 grams catfish or snakehead fish fillets
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 1 tablespoon minced shallots
- 1 tablespoon cooking oil
- Fresh cilantro for garnish
- Cooked jasmine rice for serving

Instructions:
- Cut the fish fillets into bite-sized pieces.
- In a bowl, mix together the fish sauce, sugar, minced garlic, and minced shallots.
- Heat the cooking oil in a clay pot or deep skillet over medium heat.
- Add the fish to the pot and pour the sauce mixture over it.
- Cook for a few minutes until the sauce starts to caramelize and coat the fish.
- Reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for about 15-20 minutes until the fish is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
- Garnish with fresh cilantro.
- Serve the ca kho to hot with jasmine rice.

6. Appetizer: Cha Gio (Vietnamese Spring Rolls)
Ingredients:
- Spring roll wrappers
- Ground pork
- Shrimp, peeled and chopped
- Bean thread noodles, soaked and drained
- Shredded carrots
- Finely chopped mushrooms
- Minced garlic
- Egg, beaten
- Cooking oil for frying
- Lettuce leaves and fresh herbs for serving
- Dipping sauce (nuoc cham) for serving

Instructions:
- In a bowl, mix together the ground pork, chopped shrimp, bean thread noodles, shredded carrots, chopped mushrooms, and minced garlic.
- Place a spring roll wrapper on a clean surface.
- Spoon some of the filling onto the bottom half of the wrapper.
- Fold the sides of the wrapper over the filling, then roll it up tightly.
- Brush the top edge of the wrapper with beaten egg to seal it.
- Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.
- Heat cooking oil in a deep fryer or large skillet over medium heat.
- Fry the spring rolls until golden brown and crispy.
- Remove the spring rolls from the oil and drain them on paper towels.
- Serve the cha gio spring rolls with lettuce leaves, fresh herbs, and dipping sauce.

7. Breakfast: Bahn Mi Op La (Vietnamese Fried Egg Baguette)
Ingredients:
- Baguette or Vietnamese-style bread rolls
- Eggs
- Cooking oil
- Mayonnaise
- Maggi sauce or soy sauce
- Sliced cucumber and tomato for garnish
- Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:
- Slice the baguette or bread rolls in half lengthwise.
- Heat cooking oil in a skillet over medium heat.
- Crack an egg into the skillet and cook until the whites are set but the yolk is still runny.
- Place the cooked egg on one half of the bread

.
- Spread mayonnaise on the other half of the bread and drizzle with Maggi sauce or soy sauce.
- Garnish with sliced cucumber, tomato, and chopped green onions.
- Close the sandwich and press it lightly.
- Serve the banh mi op la with a side of pickles or hot sauce.

8. Snack: Bo Bia (Vietnamese Fresh Spring Rolls with Chinese Sausage)
Ingredients:
- Rice paper wrappers
- Chinese sausage, thinly sliced
- Jicama (or turnip), julienned
- Carrots, julienned
- Fresh lettuce leaves
- Fresh mint leaves
- Fresh cilantro leaves
- Hoisin sauce for dipping

Instructions:
- Fill a large bowl with warm water. Dip one rice paper wrapper into the water and let it soften for a few seconds.
- Place the softened rice paper on a clean surface.
- Layer a few lettuce leaves, Chinese sausage slices, jicama, carrots, fresh mint leaves, and cilantro leaves on the lower half of the rice paper.
- Roll up the rice paper tightly, folding the sides in as you go.
- Repeat with the remaining ingredients.
- Serve the bo bia rolls with hoisin sauce for dipping.

9. Dinner: Bun Cha (Grilled Pork with Vermicelli Noodles)
Ingredients:
- 500 grams pork shoulder or pork belly, thinly sliced
- 1/4 cup fish sauce
- 1/4 cup sugar
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 1 tablespoon minced shallots
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- Vermicelli noodles, cooked and drained
- Fresh lettuce leaves
- Fresh herbs (mint, cilantro, Thai basil)
- Bean sprouts and sliced cucumbers for serving
- Dipping sauce (nuoc cham) for serving

Instructions:
- In a bowl, mix together the fish sauce, sugar, vegetable oil, minced garlic, minced shallots, and lime juice.
- Add the sliced pork to the marinade and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
- Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat.
- Grill the marinated pork slices until charred and cooked through, about 2-3 minutes per side.
- Remove the pork from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes.
- Arrange the cooked vermicelli noodles, fresh lettuce leaves, fresh herbs, bean sprouts, and sliced cucumbers on serving plates.
- Place the grilled pork on top of the noodles.
- Serve the bun cha with dipping sauce.

10. Dessert: Che Chuoi (Banana Coconut Sweet Soup)
Ingredients:
- 4 ripe bananas, sliced
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1 cup water
- 1/4 cup sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- Tapioca pearls or sago pearls, cooked
- Toasted sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:
- In a pot, combine the coconut milk, water, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil and stir until the sugar dissolves.
- Add the sliced bananas to the pot and simmer for a few minutes until the bananas are soft.
- Add the cooked tapioca pearls or sago pearls to the pot and stir to combine.
- Remove the pot from the heat and let the sweet soup cool slightly.
- Serve the che chuoi warm or chilled, garnished with toasted sesame seeds.
