1. Breakfast: Khao Piak Sen (Lao Chicken Noodle Soup)
Ingredients:
- 2 cups rice flour
- 1 cup tapioca flour
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 2 cups water
- 1 boneless, skinless chicken breast, thinly sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 green onions, chopped
- Fish sauce to taste
- Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:
- In a bowl, mix together the rice flour and tapioca flour.
- Gradually add water while stirring until a smooth batter is formed.
- Bring the chicken broth and water to a boil in a large pot.
- Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the boiling broth to form noodles. Cook until the noodles float to the surface.
- Add the chicken slices, minced garlic, and chopped green onions to the pot. Cook until the chicken is cooked through.
- Season with fish sauce to taste.
- Serve the khao piak sen hot, garnished with lime wedges.

2. Appetizer: Larb Gai (Lao Chicken Salad)
Ingredients:
- 1 pound ground chicken
- 2 tablespoons rice powder
- 2 tablespoons lime juice
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 tablespoon roasted chili powder
- 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
- 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
- 1/4 cup chopped green onions
- Lettuce leaves for serving

Instructions:
- In a skillet, cook the ground chicken over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Remove from heat and let it cool.
- In a bowl, mix together the rice powder, lime juice, fish sauce, sugar, and roasted chili powder.
- Add the cooked ground chicken, mint leaves, cilantro, and green onions to the bowl. Toss to combine.
- Serve the larb gai on lettuce leaves as a refreshing appetizer.

3. Lunch: Mok Pa (Lao Fish Steamed in Banana Leaves)
Ingredients:
- 1 pound white fish fillets (such as tilapia or catfish)
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 2 tablespoons lime juice
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon minced lemongrass
- 1 tablespoon minced galangal
- 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro
- Banana leaves for wrapping

Instructions:
- In a bowl, mix together the fish sauce, lime juice, sugar, minced garlic, minced lemongrass, minced galangal, and chopped cilantro to make a marinade.
- Place each fish fillet on a piece of banana leaf.
- Pour the marinade over the fish fillets, ensuring they are well coated.
- Wrap the banana leaves around the fish fillets to form packets.
- Steam the packets over high heat for about 15-20 minutes or until the fish is cooked through.
- Serve the mok pa hot, unwrapping the banana leaves before eating.

4. Snack: Ping Kai (Lao Grilled Chicken Skewers)
Ingredients:
- 1 pound chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 1 tablespoon minced lemongrass
- Bamboo skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)

Instructions:
- In a bowl, mix together the fish sauce, oyster sauce, soy sauce, sugar, minced garlic, and minced lemongrass to

 make a marinade.
- Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Let them marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor.
- Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto the soaked bamboo skewers.
- Grill the skewers over medium heat, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and nicely charred.
- Serve the ping kai hot as a flavorful snack.

5. Dinner: Or Lam (Lao Spicy Stew)
Ingredients:
- 1 pound pork ribs or beef, cut into chunks
- 2 cups water
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 1 cup eggplant, sliced
- 1 cup pumpkin, diced
- 1 cup long beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 cup bamboo shoots, sliced
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 2 tablespoons roasted chili paste
- 2 tablespoons tamarind pulp
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 1 tablespoon minced galangal
- 1 tablespoon minced lemongrass
- 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro

Instructions:
- In a large pot, bring the water and chicken broth to a boil.
- Add the pork ribs or beef chunks to the pot and simmer for about 1 hour or until the meat is tender.
- Add the eggplant, pumpkin, long beans, and bamboo shoots to the pot. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
- In a small bowl, mix together the fish sauce, roasted chili paste, tamarind pulp, sugar, minced garlic, minced galangal, and minced lemongrass.
- Add the mixture to the pot and simmer for another 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
- Stir in the chopped cilantro just before serving.
- Serve the or lam hot as a hearty stew.

6. Appetizer: Jeow Mak Len (Lao Tomato Dip)
Ingredients:
- 4-5 ripe tomatoes, roasted or grilled
- 3-4 shallots, roasted or grilled
- 2-3 cloves garlic, roasted or grilled
- 2-3 Thai chili peppers
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- 1 teaspoon sugar

Instructions:
- In a mortar and pestle, pound the roasted or grilled tomatoes, shallots, garlic, and Thai chili peppers until well combined and a chunky dip is formed.
- Add the fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar to the mortar and continue pounding until all the ingredients are well incorporated.
- Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
- Serve the jeow mak len as a spicy and tangy dip with sticky rice or grilled meats.

7. Dessert: Khao Niew Mamuang (Lao Mango Sticky Rice)
Ingredients:
- 1 cup glutinous rice
- 1 ripe mango, peeled and sliced
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1/4 cup sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- Toasted sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:
- Soak the glutinous rice in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
- Drain the rice and steam it in a bamboo steamer or a steamer basket lined with cheesecloth for about 30-40 minutes or until cooked and sticky.
- In a saucepan, heat the coconut milk, sugar, and salt over medium heat until the sugar is dissolved.
- Remove the cooked sticky rice from the steamer and transfer it to a bowl.
- Pour the coconut milk mixture over the sticky rice and let it sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
- Serve the khao niew mamuang warm or at room temperature, topped with sliced

 mango and sprinkled with toasted sesame seeds.

8. Breakfast: Khao Jee (Lao Baguette Sandwich)
Ingredients:
- Lao baguette or French baguette
- Grilled or roasted meat (such as pork, beef, or chicken), sliced
- Sliced cucumbers
- Pickled carrots and daikon
- Cilantro leaves
- Maggi seasoning sauce or soy sauce

Instructions:
- Slice the Lao baguette or French baguette lengthwise, creating a pocket for the fillings.
- Layer the grilled or roasted meat, sliced cucumbers, pickled carrots and daikon, and cilantro leaves inside the baguette.
- Drizzle with Maggi seasoning sauce or soy sauce for extra flavor.
- Serve the khao jee as a delicious and filling breakfast option.

9. Snack: Khao Poon (Lao Rice Vermicelli Soup)
Ingredients:
- 1 package rice vermicelli noodles, cooked according to package instructions
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1 cup ground chicken or pork
- 2 tablespoons red curry paste
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- Assorted vegetables (such as mushrooms, green beans, and bean sprouts)
- Fresh herbs (such as cilantro and Thai basil) for garnish
- Crispy fried noodles for topping

Instructions:
- In a pot, bring the chicken broth and coconut milk to a boil.
- Add the ground chicken or pork to the pot and cook until cooked through.
- Stir in the red curry paste, fish sauce, and lime juice.
- Add the assorted vegetables to the pot and cook until tender.
- Divide the cooked rice vermicelli noodles among serving bowls.
- Ladle the soup over the noodles and garnish with fresh herbs.
- Top with crispy fried noodles for added texture.
- Serve the khao poon hot as a flavorful and comforting snack.

10. Dinner: Sin Savanh (Lao Beef Salad)
Ingredients:
- 1 pound beef steak, grilled and thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 2 tablespoons lime juice
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 1 tablespoon minced shallots
- 1 tablespoon minced lemongrass
- 1 tablespoon chopped mint leaves
- 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro
- 1 tablespoon chopped green onions
- Lettuce leaves for serving

Instructions:
- In a bowl, mix together the fish sauce, lime juice, sugar, minced garlic, minced shallots, minced lemongrass, chopped mint leaves, chopped cilantro, and chopped green onions to make a dressing.
- Add the grilled and thinly sliced beef to the bowl. Toss to combine and allow the flavors to meld.
- Serve the sin savanh on lettuce leaves as a zesty and aromatic salad.
