Certainly! Here are ten Cambodian recipes that cover breakfast to dinner, including appetizers and desserts:

1. Breakfast - Bai Sach Chrouk (Grilled Pork and Rice):
Ingredients:
- 500g pork loin, thinly sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 4 cups cooked jasmine rice
- Sliced cucumber and scallions for garnish

Instructions:
- In a bowl, combine garlic, fish sauce, soy sauce, and sugar to make a marinade.
- Marinate the pork slices in the mixture for at least 30 minutes.
- Grill or pan-fry the pork until cooked and slightly charred.
- Serve the grilled pork over a plate of jasmine rice.
- Garnish with sliced cucumber and scallions.

2. Appetizer - Nom Banh Chok (Khmer Noodles with Fish Curry):
Ingredients:
- 500g fresh rice noodles
- 500g fish fillets, cut into chunks
- 2 tablespoons fish paste
- 2 cups coconut milk
- 2 tablespoons curry powder
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- Fresh herbs and bean sprouts for garnish

Instructions:
- Cook the fresh rice noodles according to the package instructions.
- In a pot, combine fish paste, coconut milk, curry powder, and sugar.
- Add the fish fillets to the pot and simmer until cooked.
- Place a portion of noodles in a bowl and ladle the fish curry over them.
- Garnish with fresh herbs and bean sprouts.

3. Brunch - Num Pang (Cambodian Sandwich):
Ingredients:
- Baguette or French bread
- Grilled or roasted meat (pork, chicken, or beef)
- Pickled vegetables (carrots, cucumbers, radishes)
- Fresh herbs (cilantro, basil, mint)
- Spicy mayo or chili sauce

Instructions:
- Slice the baguette or French bread lengthwise.
- Spread a layer of spicy mayo or chili sauce on the bread.
- Layer with grilled or roasted meat, pickled vegetables, and fresh herbs.
- Close the sandwich and enjoy the flavorful combination.

4. Lunch - Amok Trey (Fish Curry):
Ingredients:
- 500g fish fillets, cut into chunks
- 2 tablespoons curry paste
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- Kaffir lime leaves, thinly sliced
- Steamed jasmine rice for serving

Instructions:
- In a pan, heat the curry paste with a little oil until fragrant.
- Add coconut milk and stir well.
- Add fish sauce, sugar, and kaffir lime leaves, and mix until combined.
- Add the fish chunks to the curry mixture and cook until the fish is cooked through.
- Serve the fish curry over steamed jasmine rice.

5. Snack - Num Krok (Coconut Rice Pancakes):
Ingredients:
- 1 cup rice flour
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1/4 cup shredded coconut
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- Cooking oil for frying

Instructions:
- In a bowl, combine rice flour, coconut milk, shredded coconut, sugar, and salt. Mix well.
- Heat a non-stick pan and lightly grease it with cooking oil.
- Pour a small amount of the batter into each pancake mold on the pan.
- Cook until the edges turn golden brown, then flip and cook the other side.
- Repeat with the remaining batter.
- Serve the coconut rice pancakes as a delightful snack.

6. Dinner - Lok Lak (Marinated Beef Stir-Fry):
Ingredients:
-

 500g beef sirloin, thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- Sliced tomatoes and lettuce leaves for serving
- Steamed jasmine rice for serving

Instructions:
- In a bowl, combine soy sauce, oyster sauce, Worcestershire sauce, sugar, and garlic to make a marinade.
- Marinate the beef slices in the mixture for at least 30 minutes.
- Heat a pan or wok and stir-fry the marinated beef until cooked to your desired level of doneness.
- Serve the beef stir-fry over steamed jasmine rice.
- Garnish with sliced tomatoes and lettuce leaves.

7. Side Dish - Somlar Machu Ktiss (Cambodian Sour Soup):
Ingredients:
- 500g fish fillets, cut into chunks
- 2 cups water
- 1 cup tamarind juice
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 cup pineapple chunks
- Assorted vegetables (okra, eggplant, green beans)
- Fresh herbs for garnish (cilantro, basil)

Instructions:
- In a pot, combine water, tamarind juice, fish sauce, and sugar. Bring to a boil.
- Add fish chunks, pineapple chunks, and assorted vegetables to the pot.
- Simmer until the fish is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
- Serve the sour soup hot, garnished with fresh herbs.

8. Dessert - Kralan (Sticky Rice Cake):
Ingredients:
- 2 cups sticky rice
- 1 cup grated coconut
- 1 cup red beans, cooked
- 1/2 cup palm sugar, grated
- Banana leaves, cut into squares

Instructions:
- Soak the sticky rice in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
- Drain the rice and mix it with grated coconut and palm sugar.
- Place a portion of the sticky rice mixture on a banana leaf square.
- Add a spoonful of cooked red beans in the center.
- Fold the banana leaf to enclose the rice and beans, forming a cylindrical shape.
- Steam the rice cakes for about 30-40 minutes until cooked and firm.
- Allow to cool before serving.

9. Brunch - BawBaw (Cambodian Congee):
Ingredients:
- 1 cup jasmine rice
- 6 cups chicken or vegetable broth
- 200g ground pork or chicken
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- Sliced green onions and cilantro for garnish
- Fried shallots for garnish

Instructions:
- Rinse the jasmine rice and drain well.
- In a pot, bring the broth to a boil and add the rice.
- Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 45 minutes until the rice is soft and the congee is thickened.
- In a separate pan, cook the ground pork or chicken with garlic, soy sauce, and fish sauce until cooked through.
- Serve the congee in bowls, topped with the cooked ground meat.
- Garnish with sliced green onions, cilantro, and fried shallots.

10. Dessert - Num Ansom Chek (Banana Sticky Rice in Banana Leaf):
Ingredients:
- 2 cups sticky rice
- 4 ripe bananas, sliced
- 1/2 cup grated coconut
- 1/4 cup palm sugar, grated
- Banana leaves, cut into squares

Instructions:
- Soak the sticky rice in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
- Drain the rice and steam it

 until partially cooked.
- Mix the partially cooked sticky rice with grated coconut and palm sugar.
- Place a portion of the rice mixture on a banana leaf square.
- Add a few slices of banana on top of the rice.
- Fold the banana leaf to enclose the rice and banana, forming a rectangular shape.
- Steam the banana leaf packets for about 30-40 minutes until the rice is fully cooked.
- Allow to cool before serving.
