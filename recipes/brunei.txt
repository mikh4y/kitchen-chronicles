Brunei Recipes 
Certainly! Here are ten Bruneian recipes that cover breakfast to dinner, including appetizers and desserts:

1. Breakfast - Ambuyat:
Ingredients:
- 250g sago starch
- 1 liter water
- Sambal belacan (chili shrimp paste dip)
- Grilled fish or chicken (optional)

Instructions:
- In a large pot, bring water to a boil.
- Slowly add sago starch while stirring continuously until it thickens into a sticky, glue-like consistency.
- Remove from heat and transfer to a serving dish.
- Serve with sambal belacan and grilled fish or chicken.

2. Appetizer - Prawn Sambal:
Ingredients:
- 500g prawns, peeled and deveined
- 4 shallots, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 red chilies, sliced
- 2 tablespoons tamarind juice
- 1 tablespoon palm sugar
- Salt to taste
- Cooking oil for frying

Instructions:
- Heat oil in a pan and sauté shallots, garlic, and red chilies until fragrant.
- Add prawns and cook until they turn pink and are cooked through.
- Stir in tamarind juice, palm sugar, and salt.
- Cook for a few more minutes until the sauce thickens.
- Serve as an appetizer with steamed rice or as part of a meal.

3. Brunch - Roti Canai with Curry:
Ingredients:
- 500g all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
- Water, as needed
- Curry sauce (chicken, beef, or vegetable curry)

Instructions:
- In a mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, and ghee or vegetable oil.
- Gradually add water and knead until the dough is smooth and elastic.
- Divide the dough into small balls and flatten them into thin rounds.
- Heat a griddle or skillet and cook the flattened dough on both sides until golden brown.
- Serve with curry sauce for dipping.

4. Lunch - Nasi Lemak (Coconut Rice with Accompaniments):
Ingredients:
- 2 cups jasmine rice
- 1 cup coconut milk
- Pandan leaves, tied into a knot
- Fried anchovies
- Sliced cucumber
- Hard-boiled eggs
- Sambal (spicy chili paste)
- Roasted peanuts

Instructions:
- Rinse rice and drain well.
- In a rice cooker, add rice, coconut milk, pandan leaves, and water (in the ratio 1:1).
- Cook the rice as usual.
- Serve the coconut rice with fried anchovies, sliced cucumber, hard-boiled eggs, sambal, and roasted peanuts.

5. Snack - Kueh Mor (Steamed Banana Cake):
Ingredients:
- 3 ripe bananas, mashed
- 200g rice flour
- 100g tapioca flour
- 150g sugar
- 200ml coconut milk
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- Banana leaves, cut into small squares

Instructions:
- In a mixing bowl, combine mashed bananas, rice flour, tapioca flour, sugar, coconut milk, and baking powder. Mix well.
- Grease the banana leaf squares with oil.
- Spoon the batter onto the greased banana leaf squares.
- Steam the kueh mor for about 20-25 minutes until cooked through.
- Allow to cool before serving.

6. Dinner - Ikan Asam Pedas (Spicy Sour Fish Stew):
Ingredients:
- 500g fish fillets (snapper, mackerel, or any firm fish)
- 4 shallots, chopped
- 4

 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 red chilies, sliced
- 2 tablespoons tamarind juice
- 1 tablespoon palm sugar
- Salt to taste
- Cooking oil for frying

Instructions:
- Heat oil in a pan and sauté shallots, garlic, and red chilies until fragrant.
- Add fish fillets and cook until they are lightly browned.
- Stir in tamarind juice, palm sugar, and salt.
- Add water as needed to create a stew-like consistency.
- Simmer for a few minutes until the fish is cooked through and the flavors meld together.
- Serve with steamed rice.

7. Side Dish - Acar (Spicy Pickled Vegetables):
Ingredients:
- 1 cucumber, julienned
- 1 carrot, julienned
- 1 red chili, sliced
- 4 shallots, thinly sliced
- 1 cup white vinegar
- 1 cup water
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions:
- In a saucepan, combine vinegar, water, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil and stir until sugar and salt dissolve.
- Place cucumber, carrot, red chili, and shallots in a heat-resistant jar or bowl.
- Pour the hot vinegar mixture over the vegetables.
- Let it cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least a few hours or overnight.
- Serve as a tangy side dish with any meal.

8. Dessert - Kuih Lapis (Layered Rice Cake):
Ingredients:
- 200g rice flour
- 200ml coconut milk
- 200ml water
- 150g sugar
- Food coloring (pandan, red, and yellow)
- Banana leaves, cut into squares

Instructions:
- In a mixing bowl, combine rice flour, coconut milk, water, and sugar. Mix well.
- Divide the batter into three equal portions.
- Add different food colorings to each portion, creating three different colors.
- Grease a square or round baking dish and line it with banana leaves.
- Pour a layer of one colored batter into the dish and steam for a few minutes until set.
- Repeat with the other two colored batters, layering and steaming until all the batter is used.
- Allow to cool before slicing into squares or diamonds.

9. Brunch - Mee Siput (Spicy Snail Noodles):
Ingredients:
- 200g fresh snails
- 200g yellow noodles
- 100g bean sprouts
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons cooking oil
- 1 tablespoon chili paste
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:
- Boil the snails in water until cooked and tender. Remove from shells and set aside.
- Cook yellow noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
- In a pan, heat oil and sauté minced garlic until fragrant.
- Add snails, bean sprouts, and chili paste. Stir-fry for a few minutes.
- Add cooked yellow noodles and soy sauce. Mix well.
- Season with salt and pepper to taste.
- Serve as a spicy and flavorful brunch option.

10. Dessert - Pengat Pisang (Banana in Coconut Milk):
Ingredients:
- 4 ripe bananas, sliced
- 200ml coconut milk
- 100g palm sugar, grated
- 2 pandan leaves, tied into a knot
- 1 tablespoon rice flour, dissolved in water

Instructions:
- In a saucepan, heat coconut milk, palm sugar, and pandan leaves over medium heat.
- Stir until the palm sugar dissolves and the mixture

 comes to a simmer.
- Add the sliced bananas and continue cooking until the bananas are soft.
- Stir in the rice flour mixture to thicken the coconut milk.
- Simmer for a few more minutes, then remove from heat.
- Serve warm or chilled as a delightful dessert.
