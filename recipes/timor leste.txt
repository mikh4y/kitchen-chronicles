1. Breakfast - Batar Da'an (Corn Porridge):
Ingredients:
- 1 cup cornmeal
- 4 cups water
- 1 cup coconut milk
- Sugar or honey to taste
- Pinch of salt

Instructions:
- In a pot, bring water to a boil.
- Slowly whisk in the cornmeal, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
- Reduce heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
- Stir in coconut milk, sugar or honey, and salt.
- Continue to simmer for another 5 minutes until the porridge thickens.
- Serve hot as a comforting breakfast dish.

2. Appetizer - Tapai (Fermented Cassava):
Ingredients:
- 500g cassava, peeled and grated
- Banana leaves for wrapping
- Water for soaking
- Fermented rice (optional)

Instructions:
- Soak the grated cassava in water overnight.
- Drain and squeeze out any excess liquid from the cassava.
- Place a portion of cassava on a piece of banana leaf and fold it into a packet.
- Repeat with the remaining cassava.
- Place the wrapped cassava packets in a cool, dark place for 2-3 days to ferment.
- Serve tapai as an appetizer or snack, optionally pairing it with fermented rice.

3. Brunch - Tukir (Savoury Rice Porridge):
Ingredients:
- 1 cup jasmine rice
- 4 cups water or chicken broth
- 200g chicken, cooked and shredded
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons cooking oil
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Sliced scallions for garnish

Instructions:
- Rinse the jasmine rice and drain well.
- In a pot, bring water or chicken broth to a boil.
- Add the rice and reduce heat to low.
- Simmer for about 30 minutes until the rice is soft and the mixture thickens.
- In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté minced garlic until fragrant.
- Add shredded chicken and cook until heated through.
- Stir in soy sauce and season with salt and pepper.
- Serve the rice porridge topped with the chicken mixture.
- Garnish with sliced scallions.

4. Lunch - Ikan Saboko (Grilled Fish with Sambal):
Ingredients:
- 500g fish (mackerel or snapper), cleaned and gutted
- 3 red chilies, seeded and chopped
- 2 shallots, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- Juice of 1 lime
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Cooking oil for grilling

Instructions:
- Preheat the grill or a barbecue.
- Make a paste by blending together red chilies, shallots, garlic, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
- Score the fish on both sides.
- Rub the chili paste onto the fish, ensuring it is well coated.
- Grill the fish for about 6-8 minutes on each side until it is cooked through and charred.
- Serve the grilled fish with steamed rice and sambal.

5. Snack - Batar Patis (Corn Fritters):
Ingredients:
- 2 cups corn kernels
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- Cooking oil for frying

Instructions:
- In a bowl, combine corn kernels, all-purpose flour, beaten eggs, baking powder, and salt.
- Mix well until a thick batter forms.
- Heat

 oil in a pan over medium heat.
- Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil and flatten slightly with the back of the spoon.
- Fry until golden brown on both sides.
- Remove the fritters from the oil and drain on paper towels.
- Serve the corn fritters as a delicious snack.

6. Dinner - Xefe Xefe (Timorese Beef Stew):
Ingredients:
- 500g beef, cut into chunks
- 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
- 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tomatoes, diced
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 2 tablespoons cooking oil
- 4 cups beef broth
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:
- Heat oil in a pot over medium heat.
- Sauté chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant.
- Add beef chunks and cook until browned on all sides.
- Stir in tomato paste and diced tomatoes.
- Pour in beef broth and bring to a boil.
- Reduce heat to low and simmer for about 1 hour until the beef is tender.
- Add potatoes and carrots to the pot and continue simmering until the vegetables are cooked.
- Season with salt and pepper.
- Serve the beef stew hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.

7. Side Dish - Kankong Belak (Stir-Fried Water Spinach):
Ingredients:
- 500g water spinach (kankong), cleaned and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Instructions:
- Heat oil in a wok or pan over high heat.
- Sauté minced garlic until fragrant.
- Add water spinach and stir-fry for a few minutes until wilted.
- Stir in oyster sauce and soy sauce.
- Continue cooking for another minute or until the water spinach is cooked but still crisp.
- Serve the stir-fried water spinach as a side dish.

8. Dessert - Sagu Belik (Tapioca Pudding):
Ingredients:
- 1 cup small tapioca pearls
- 4 cups water
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1/2 cup palm sugar, grated
- Pinch of salt

Instructions:
- In a pot, bring water to a boil.
- Add tapioca pearls and cook until they are translucent and soft.
- Drain the cooked tapioca pearls and rinse with cold water.
- In a separate pot, heat coconut milk, palm sugar, and salt, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
- Add the cooked tapioca pearls to the coconut milk mixture and stir well.
- Cook for a few more minutes until the mixture thickens slightly.
- Serve the tapioca pudding warm or chilled.

9. Brunch - Belekan (Timorese Pancakes):
Ingredients:
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1/2 cup water
- 2 eggs
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
- Cooking oil for frying

Instructions:
- In a bowl, whisk together all-purpose flour, coconut milk, water, eggs, sugar, and baking powder until well combined.
- Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
- Pour a ladleful of the pancake batter onto the hot pan and spread it into a thin round shape.
- Cook until golden brown on one side, then flip and cook the other side.
- Repeat with the remaining batter.
- Serve the Timorese pancakes with your choice of toppings such as honey or jam.



10. Dessert - Bibinka (Coconut Rice Cake):
Ingredients:
- 2 cups glutinous rice
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1 cup water
- 1/2 cup sugar
- Banana leaves for lining
- Grated coconut for garnish

Instructions:
- Rinse the glutinous rice and drain well.
- In a pot, combine glutinous rice, coconut milk, water, and sugar.
- Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the rice is fully cooked and the mixture thickens.
- Line a baking dish with banana leaves and transfer the cooked rice mixture into the dish.
- Smooth the top with a spatula.
- Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for about 20-25 minutes until the top is golden brown.
- Remove from the oven and let it cool.
- Cut into squares or diamond shapes and garnish with grated coconut.
